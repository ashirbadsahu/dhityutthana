what is dhityutthana?

" धीति + उत्थान = धित्युत्थान "
" Dhiti + Utthana = Dhityutthana "
 dhiti means wisdom and utthana means rise. hence, the whole word dhityutthana means rise of wisdom.

    • Vedas are oldest text in Bharatiya culture and some hydrological evidence indicates that they are composed before 2000BC, but their origin of time haven't been known. They were originally transmitted from one person to another by speech and it was remembered by the people so it is also called "Shruti". However, there are 4 Vedas i.e., Rig Veda the oldest one talks, Sama Veda on prayers and music, Yajur Veda on politics and economics, Athrava Veda on various knowledges. They are divided on various Mandals and Sutras.
    Upanishads are more like philosophical teaching of vedas with it's own elements. They represent knowledge Bramhan. "What is this world? Who am I? Meaning of life, What happens after death?" such questions are answered in Upanishads. There are over 200 Upanishads but the traditional number is 108. Among them, 10 are principal Upanishads. They are Isha, Kena, Katha, Prashan, Mundaka, Mandukya, Tattiriya, Aitareya, Chhandogya and Brihadaranyaka
    Shrimad Bhawat Gita is the knowledge given by himself Bhagwan Shri Krishna to Arjuna in Mahabharata war. Arjuna wanted to give up on war cause he was unable to fight against his relatives and friends Bhagwan Shri Krishna was driving his chariot, explained Arujna why he should fight. This dialogue between Arjuna and Shri Krishna is extremely knowledgeful and useful at the same time.
    Ramayana is the life description of Maryada Purushottam Shri Ram. Composed by Maharshi Balmiki, it has 6 kandas, namely, Bala Kanda, Ayodhya Kanda, Aranya Kanda, Kishkindhya Kanda, Sundara Kanda, Yuddha Kanda which are further divided into various sargas. Shri Ram is one of the Avataras of Vishnu and his wife Sita was herself Laxmi. Along with Maa Sita and Pravu Shri Ram we can see Bhagwan Hanumana, Maharaj Laxman, Maharaj Bharat and a lot other people's description in Ramayana from whom we can learn from.
    Mahabharata and Ramayana are considered as Itihasa epics. It is the longest epic in the world. Mahabharata was composed by Maharshi Vyasadeva, contains 18 parvas consisting of over 100,000 shlokas. It contains description of mostly Kuru Dynasty(the dynasty in which Pandavas and Kauravs were born) and Bhagwan Shri Krishna along with other descriptions.
    Bhashyas are the commenteries on various texts by great Sages. They simplify the teachings of main texts in a simpler language. Many Sages like Adi Shankaracharya, Narada, Ramanujacharya, Madhvacharya, Maharshi Patanjali and in modern time Swami Vivekanada with thier teaching have played key role in strengthing the core philosphy and teaching of scriptures.
